AUTOMATION AND ROBOTICS ENGINEERING

Automatic and robotics refer to the use of technology to automate or augment tasks that are typically performed by humans. Automatic systems are those that can operate without human intervention, using sensors, algorithms, and other software or hardware components to perform tasks. Examples of automatic systems include self-driving cars, automated manufacturing equipment, and smart home systems. Robotics, on the other hand, refers to the use of robots to perform tasks. Robots are programmable machines that can perform a wide range of tasks, from simple repetitive actions to complex movements and decision-making processes. Robotics has applications in manufacturing, healthcare, education, and many other fields. Both automatic systems and robotics have the potential to increase efficiency, accuracy, and safety in a variety of industries. However, there are also concerns about the impact of these technologies on employment and the economy, as well as ethical considerations related to the use of artificial intelligence and autonomous systems.

AUTOMATION AND ROBOTICS ENGINEERING AT SITRC

SITRC established a department dedicated to automation and robotics engineering under the aegis of the board of mechanical engineering from the academic year 2020-2021. The program has sanctioned intake of 60 and is affiliated to SPPU, Pune. The program will build the engineers through interdisciplinary studies encompassing computer engineering, mechanical engineering and electrical engineering. The department is composed of dedicated senior faculty members from the core field to conduct rigorous academics. Also, there will be a blend of faculties from other streams as per the demand of curriculum. The department has procured a robotic arm for the demonstration purpose. Also, there are established labs for Mechatronics as well as hydraulics and pneumatics to give practical insight to the students on sensors and actuators. The department has nurtured a culture of closer industry-institute interactions. There are compulsory visits to various industries which have implemented higher level automations.
